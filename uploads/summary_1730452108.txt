This article discusses the concept of wikis, which are a form of hypertext publication on the internet that can be collaboratively edited and managed by its audience through a web browser. The article defines the characteristics of wikis, such as their multiple pages that can be edited by the public or limited to use within an organization. Wiki engines, which power wikis, are also discussed, including their content management system, lightweight markup language, and little inherent structure. The article provides examples of the legal environment, communities, and growth factors of wikis. Additionally, it covers the trust, security, and hosting of wikis. The article concludes with a discussion of the history and alternative definitions of wikis, as well as references for further reading.
